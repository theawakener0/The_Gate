Project Overview: Next-Generation Assistive Robotics for the Visually Impaired

At ISEF, our project presents an innovative, interdisciplinary solution designed to empower visually impaired individuals by seamlessly integrating robotics, artificial intelligence, and advanced health monitoring. Our team—comprising Ahmed Khaled and Omar Ashraf—has combined our diverse expertise to create a system that not only assists with navigation and everyday tasks but also prioritizes user safety and health.


Interdisciplinary Approach and Impact:

By merging robotics, AI, and health technology, our project addresses both mobility and well-being. The assistive robot not only aids in navigation and everyday tasks but also provides a critical health monitoring solution—bridging the gap between independent living and continuous care. This holistic approach reflects our commitment to enhancing quality of life and promoting social inclusivity.


Our project at ISEF embodies a fusion of technical innovation and social impact, demonstrating how emerging technologies can be harnessed to create real-world solutions for vulnerable communities. Through continuous refinement and testing, we aim to set new standards in assistive robotics and pave the way for future advancements in accessible technology.
